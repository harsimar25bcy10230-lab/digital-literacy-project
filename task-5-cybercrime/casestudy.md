## Phishing Attack Case Study

Phishing is a cybercrime where attackers impersonate trusted entities to steal sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs.

A typical phishing attack begins with a fake email or message that appears to come from a legitimate source such as a bank or service provider. The message creates urgency, such as claiming account suspension or suspicious activity. The user is then asked to click a link, which redirects to a fake website designed to look real.

Once the user enters their credentials, the attacker captures the information and gains access to the account. In many cases, attackers immediately use this access to perform unauthorized transactions or further attacks.

Students are often targeted due to lower awareness and frequent use of online platforms. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and account compromise.

This highlights the importance of verifying links and avoiding sharing sensitive information online.
